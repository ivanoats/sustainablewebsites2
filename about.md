# About

## A Twenty-Year Mission

In 2004, the conversation around "green tech" was just beginning. I founded
Sustainable Websites with a simple but ambitious goal: to provide a livelihood
that was as environmentally responsible as it was technically sound. At the
time, that meant pioneering carbon-offset web hosting, thus bridging the gap
between the digital world and the physical planet.

In 2013, after a decade of managing servers, I made a conscious choice to pivot.
I sold the hosting business to
[GreenGeeks](https://www.greengeeks.com/track/sustainableweb/cp-sw2)
to focus on two other passions: raising my children and teaching the next
generation of developers.

## The Evolution of Scale

Sustainability is as much about people and processes as it is about power usage.
I founded [CodeFellows](https://www.codefellows.org/)
to create a more sustainable model for technical education, moving away from the
"gig economy" feel of adjunct professorship to build a lasting ecosystem for
knowledge sharing.

For the last six years, I have served as a Senior Software Engineer and
Architect at
[lululemon](https://shop.lululemon.com/). There, I've
operated at the highest levels of digital scale, learning how to optimize web
development teams, streamline complex processes, and architect systems that
serve millions.

## The New Standard: Beyond Offsets

With the recent release of the
[W3C Web Sustainability Guidelines](https://www.w3.org/TR/web-sustainability-guidelines/)
(WSG), the industry has reached a turning
point. We now have the framework to move beyond simple carbon offsets and
address the root of the problem: digital waste.

The urgency is real. The digital industry is now responsible for 2-5% of global
emissions. If the internet were a country, it would rank among the top five
polluters in the world. Between 2015 and 2021, internet visitors increased 60%,
while web traffic increased by 440%. Since the Paris Agreement, average page
sizes have increased by over 70% on desktop and 140% on mobile.[^1]

These aren't distant problems—they're immediate challenges that every
organization building digital products must face today.

True sustainability is found in efficiency. It’s in the architecture of our
code, the weight of our pages, and the logic of our development processes.
